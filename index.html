Tax Credit — Do This Every Hire
Resource #14

Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC)

Hiring someone with a disability, a veteran, or a long-term unemployed person? The federal WOTC gives you a tax credit of $2,400–$9,600 per qualifying hire — real money for a small business. It's filed with your taxes, costs nothing to apply for, and your accountant can claim it retroactively. Eligible categories: people with disabilities, veterans, SNAP recipients, ex-felons, long-term unemployed, summer youth employees. How: work with your payroll provider (Square, Gusto, etc.) or file IRS Form 8850 within 28 days of hire.
Verdict: DO THIS (every hire)
dol.gov/agencies/eta/wotc

What wraps around this hire
This isn't charity — it's infrastructure. The support system exists specifically so that hiring someone with a disability isn't harder than hiring anyone else. Most employers never access it because they don't know it's there.
Once you make a qualifying hire, these free services kick in to help them succeed:
Job coaching (Vocational Rehabilitation / VR)
The Arizona Division of Vocational Rehabilitation (azdes.gov/vr) provides free on-site job coaches for employees with disabilities — someone who comes to your location and helps the employee learn the job, adapt workflows, and build independence. Zero cost to you as the employer.
Assistive technology funding
VR can also fund equipment or modifications that help the employee do the job — ergonomic tools, adaptive software, specialized equipment. You request it; they pay for it.
ADA tax credit (separate from WOTC)
Small businesses with under 30 employees or under $1M revenue can claim an additional 50% tax credit (up to $5,000/year) for costs of making accommodations — ramps, equipment, accessible workstations. IRS Form 8826. Stacks with WOTC.
Job Accommodation Network (JAN)
Free consulting from askjan.org (federally funded) on exactly what accommodations work for specific conditions. You describe the job and the employee's situation; they give you specific, tested solutions. Completely free, no strings.
Benefits counseling for the employee
Employees coming off SSI/SSDI are often afraid to take a job because they think they'll lose benefits — this causes them to decline offers or quit early. Arizona ADES Work Incentives Planning & Assistance (WIPA) provides free counseling on how work affects their benefits. Ask azdes.gov to connect the employee with a WIPA counselor before day one.
On-the-Job Training (OJT) subsidy
Through Arizona@Work, you may qualify for a wage reimbursement of 50–75% of the employee's wages for the first 90 days while they're training. Designed exactly for this scenario.
Check-in support
Most VR cases have an ongoing counselor assigned. If the employee is struggling after 30/60/90 days, call their VR counselor — that's what they're there for. You are not alone in making this work.
